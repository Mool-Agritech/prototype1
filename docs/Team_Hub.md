## ✅ What we submit

- **3-minute pitch video (primary)** — problem · solution · market opportunity · business model · why this team. Max 3 min, uploaded to YouTube (set to *not private*), ideally whole team on screen.
- **150-word summary** — used for first-round judging; states problem, solution, and impact.
- **Proof-of-concept demo (secondary — "cherry on top")** — screen recording of the web platform / multi-party workflow; not a live clickable prototype.

## 🏆 How we're judged — 25 pts, ≥3 independent judges

| Criteria (5 pts each) | What judges look for |
| --- | --- |
| **1. Problem** | Human-centric; demand real, urgent, data-backed |
| **2. Solution** | Addresses the problem; new/differentiated capabilities; viable |
| **3. Level of Innovation** | Original idea + business model; use of AI/ML/DLT; how data is leveraged |
| **4. Impact** | Targets **UN SDG 13**; market size justifies upfront investment |
| **5. Team** | Right team + skills; names gaps and who fills them |

## More about the Challenge

Your task is to come up with a technology-driven product or service solution that contributes to advancing UN SDG 3 (Health), UN SDG 9 (Innovation & Infrastructure), UN SDG 11 (Cities), and SDG 13 (Climate).
The following are general guidelines to follow during the generation of the technology-driven
product or service solution :

1. The solution must be targeted to one of the four tracks of the challenge. If your idea aligns with more than one track, pick the track you feel is best suited.
2. The solution must be market-targeted. Define a geographical location to launch the product or service. Take into consideration each region’s rules and regulations. eg, Likely, an idea in Ghana may not work best for Canada due to different energy infrastructure and challenges.
3. Finished or in-process technical products or services are not compulsory. Your team is not
required to come up with any built technical product; rather, it should be a well-rounded
business concept 
4. It may be a social enterprise, however you should define the funding and business model

## 🎯 MVP scope (proof-of-concept)

<aside>
⚙️

**Guiding principle:** business model & pitch are the primary deliverables; the technical build is **proof-of-concept only** — any working dataset is acceptable, highest-quality data not required at this stage.

</aside>

**In scope**

- **Backtest — Vidarbha (Maharashtra) primary pilot** — satellite + weather data simulating parametric triggers vs. historical PMFBY payouts → quantify basis-risk reduction. Other districts (Ratnagiri as noisy-data robustness test; Rajasthan, MP, Haryana, Bihar) referenced briefly for breadth. *(Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana — India's government crop-insurance program)*
- **Two models only — backtest (simple regression) + nowcast (arithmetic); no forecast.** Yield data to be aggregated down to **polygon level** (not district). 4–5 satellite sources, region-specific features by farmer pain points.
- **Risk scoring at land/plot level** (not individual farmers) — avoids dependency on proprietary income, credit history, or tax data.
- **Web platform demo (screen recording)** — visually appealing walkthrough of the multi-party workflow (how farmers, insurers, NBFCs, input suppliers who didn't previously collaborate now work together); submitted as a screen recording, not a live clickable prototype.
- **3-min pitch video + deck (primary)** aligned to the 5 judging criteria and location-specific rules / SDG framing — incl. WhatsApp farmer-interface concept (multilingual, voice-enabled; described, not built).
- **Business model + partner memo** — polished memo for NBFCs, insurers, satellite/data providers; outreach begins immediately.

**Out of scope (state explicitly to judges)**

- Live farmer enrollment, real insurer/NBFC integration, regulatory licensing, and any forecast model.
